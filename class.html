->Career options After 12th Commerce


Here is a list of courses after 12th Commerce which you can consider if you want to build a career in finance, economics or related fields.

1. Bachelor of Commerce (BCom)



One of the most popular commerce career options is The Bachelor of Commerce (BCom) degree is a three-year undergraduate degree program in commerce and business studies. The BCom degree program covers various aspects of commerce and business, such as accounting, finance, marketing, human resources, and Information Technology. The BCom degree program is designed to provide students with a sound understanding of the principles and practices of commerce and business. 

2. Bachelor of Economics (BE)



The Bachelor of Economics (BE) is a three-year undergraduate degree in economics offered by many universities in India, Australia, and New Zealand. The BE degree is also offered by some universities in Canada, Ireland, Singapore, and the United Kingdom. The BE degree is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in economic principles and their applications. The degree covers a broad range of economic topics, including microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, and economic history. Students who complete a BE degree will have the skills and knowledge to pursue a career in economics, finance, or a related field. Obtaining this degree is among the excellent career options after 12th commerce.

3. Bachelor of Accounting and Finance (BAF)



The Bachelor of Accounting and Finance (BAF) degree is a four-year undergraduate degree that combines coursework in accounting and finance. The BAF is among professional courses after 12th commerce, designed for students who are interested in pursuing a career in accounting or finance or who wish to combine their accounting and finance knowledge to pursue a career in business. 

4. Bachelor of Commerce in Banking and Insurance (BBI)



There are many career options for commerce students. Bachelor of Commerce in Banking and Insurance (BBI) is a four-year undergraduate degree program. The program is designed to provide students with a sound understanding of the banking and insurance sectors with a focus on the Indian context. The program is also designed to develop students' analytical and problem-solving skills. 

5. Bachelor of Commerce in Financial Market (BFM)



The Bachelor of Commerce in Financial Market (BFM) is a three-year undergraduate degree. The program is designed to provide students with a sound understanding of the financial markets, and how they work. The program is highly sought-after by students who wish to enter the financial sector and has a strong track record of placing students in top-tier banks and financial institutions. This is one of the best career options after 12th commerce.

6. Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)



The Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) is a commerce stream career option which is a four-year degree program that provides students with the skills and knowledge necessary to pursue a career in business. The BBA curriculum includes courses in accounting, finance, marketing, and management. In addition to academic coursework, BBA students must complete a professional internship to graduate. 

7. Bachelor of Business Administration – International Business (BBA-IB)



The Bachelor of Business Administration – International Business (BBA-IB) degree program is designed to provide students with the necessary skills and knowledge to be successful in today’s global business environment. The program focuses on the development of cross-cultural understanding, global business knowledge, and international business skills. 

 

8. Bachelor of Business Administration – Computer Application (BBA-CA)



BBA-CA is a 3-year undergraduate course offered by many universities in India. The course is designed to provide students with an understanding of the various aspects of computer applications in businesses. The course curriculum covers topics such as programming, data structures, database management, systems analysis and design, and web technologies. 

9. Industry Oriented Integrated Courses



Industry Oriented Integrated Courses (IOICs) is an excellent commerce career option to gain the skills and knowledge needed to be successful in today's competitive marketplace. IOCs are offered by many colleges and universities and are designed to provide students with the opportunity to learn about a specific industry while also completing their degree. 

10. Chartered Accountancy



A Chartered Accountant (CA) is a popular career option after 12th commerce. It is a professional accountant degree that has been granted chartered status by a regulatory body. The role of a Chartered Accountant is to provide financial and business advice to clients. Chartered Accountants work in a variety of industries, including public accounting, corporate accounting, and government accounting. They may also work in financial services, such as investment banking and insurance.
